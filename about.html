About the island

It's history. It's culture. It's people. It's beauty.

HISTORY

Siquijor, often called the "Mystic Island of the Philippines," has a rich history filled with folklore, colonial influences, and natural wonders. Before the arrival of the Spaniards, the island was home to native Visayan people who believed in spirits and mystical powers. According to local legends, Siquijor rose from the sea after a massive earthquake and lightning storm, which is why the Spanish later called it "Isla del Fuego" (Island of Fire) due to the glowing fireflies that surrounded it.

TODAY

Today, Siquijor is famous for its natural beauty and mystical reputation. The island is home to stunning beaches, waterfalls, and snorkeling spots.

In recent years, Siquijor has gained popularity through social media, travel blogs, and vlogs, attracting tourists who seek adventure, relaxation, and spiritual experiences. Unlike other crowded tourist destinations, Siquijor remains peaceful and affordable, making it a hidden gem in the Philippines.
